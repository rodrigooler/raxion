The Root Problem

WHO JUDGES
THE JUDGE?

Every decentralized AI network today — including Bittensor — solves the same problem the same wrong way: they pay humans to judge AI quality. But humans with money on the line don't evaluate honestly. They form cartels, copy each other, and reward whichever model makes them look correct — not whichever model is actually right.

Use Case 01 — Smart Contract Security
Without RAXION

You deploy a $10M DeFi protocol. You pay a security firm $50K to audit it. The firm is incentivized to say it's fine — conflict of interest baked in. You ship. You get hacked.

With RAXION

Three heterogeneous AI agents independently analyze the contract. If all three converge on "critical reentrancy in line 247" — that result is cryptographically proven on-chain. No auditor. No conflict. Math.

Use Case 02 — Medical Research
Without RAXION

A research team asks 5 different LLMs the same drug interaction question. Gets 5 different answers. No way to know which one to trust. Falls back on a slow, expensive human specialist.

With RAXION

The network runs three architectures with complementary failure modes. Convergence produces a CoherenceScore. If the score exceeds 0.85, the answer is HIGH_CONFIDENCE — and that confidence is provably computed, not claimed.

Use Case 03 — Decentralized AI Marketplace
Without RAXION (Bittensor)

Miners earn $TAO by making validators happy. Top validators form cartels. Miners just proxy GPT-4 and pretend it's their model. Network pays millions in rewards for zero original intelligence.

With RAXION

$RAX rewards are tied to CoherenceScore — not to validator opinion. If you proxy another model, convergence still works, but stochastic verification (1.5% challenge rate) will catch you. Slashing is automatic. Math is the judge.
